Required tools/dependencies:
- Calendar-specific profile types for appointments, reschedules, conflict checks, and follow-ups.
- Date and time normalization that can support reminders and conflict detection.

Recommended tools/dependencies:
- Fixtures for single-person, couple, and family events.
- Conflict-detection tests for overlapping appointments and reschedules.

Technologies and integrations:
- CalDAV server: Local CalDAV endpoint (via sabre/dav-inspired Node.js implementation, or a library like caldav-adapter) that external calendar apps (Apple Calendar, Google Calendar, etc.) subscribe to.
- The system is the calendar source — it serves events outward, not consuming an external calendar API.
- iCal generation: ical-generator or ical.js for producing .ics feeds from CalendarState records.
- iCal parsing: ical.js or node-ical for parsing .ics attachments from forwarded emails.
- Calendar input: Events enter via conversational input (messages parsed by Claude API) or email parsing (data ingest service extracts appointment confirmations).
- Date/time handling: date-fns or Temporal (stage 3) for timezone-aware date arithmetic, conflict detection, and reminder offset calculations.
- Conflict detection: Compare event time ranges against existing CalendarState entries — pure logic, no external service needed.
- Reminders: BullMQ delayed jobs scheduled at reminder offsets (e.g., 30 min before, 1 day before) via the scheduler service.
- Follow-ups: Post-appointment follow-up enqueued via the scheduler after the event end time.
- State: CalendarState stored in SQLite (state service) with CalendarEvent records; CalDAV endpoint reads from this store.
