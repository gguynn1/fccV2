Required tools/dependencies:
- Node.js (LTS) with TypeScript (strict, ESM) for all runtime modules.
- `src/types.ts` for shared enums and cross-service vocabulary.
- Respect service and type boundaries: no direct runtime imports from sibling services.
- Local `types.ts` in this folder for scope-owned contracts.
- Calendar-specific profile types for appointments, reschedules, conflict checks, and follow-ups.
- Date and time normalization that can support reminders and conflict detection.

Recommended tools/dependencies:
- `tsc --noEmit` to verify type boundaries.
- `eslint` to keep imports and unused symbols clean.
- `prettier` to keep docs and source formatting consistent.
- `vitest` for fixture-driven verification where the scope has behavior worth testing.
- Fixtures for single-person, couple, and family events.
- Conflict-detection tests for overlapping appointments and reschedules.

Technologies and integrations:
- Calendar records persist through the state service into SQLite.
- Inbound calendar data should come from email parsing, `.ics` handling, or conversation rather than any external calendar API.
- CalDAV endpoint: the system serves a local CalDAV endpoint on a separate port (CALDAV_PORT, default 3001) accessible only on the local network — not tunneled through ngrok. Calendar apps on the home network subscribe to it.
- BullMQ scheduling supports reminders and post-appointment follow-ups.
- Recovery after downtime: reminders that should have fired during an outage are checked for staleness on startup — past-due reminders are logged, not dispatched late.
