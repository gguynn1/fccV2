Required tools/dependencies:
- Photography lead-pipeline types for inquiries, draft replies, booking status, and quiet-period follow-up.
- Approval-gated drafting support before anything is sent on behalf of PARTICIPANT 2.

Recommended tools/dependencies:
- Fixtures for new leads, stalled leads, and booking transitions.
- Tests that separate client-facing draft tone from internal assistant guidance.

Technologies and integrations:
- State: PhotographyState stored in SQLite (state service) — photo leads with status, contact info, and pipeline stage.
- Internal CRM schema: A simple, purpose-built lead-tracking schema owned by this topic — no external CRM service (no HoneyBook, Dubsado, or 17hats). Schema covers: lead contact, inquiry date, event details, pipeline stage, follow-up history, and booking status.
- Lead ingestion: Data ingest service monitors a photography-specific inbox; Claude API extracts inquiry details (date, event type, budget) from incoming emails.
- Draft composition: Claude API generates professional, warm client-facing draft replies; staged in PARTICIPANT 2's private thread for review and approval before sending.
- Approval gating: Confirmation service gates all client-facing sends — nothing goes out without PARTICIPANT 2's explicit approval.
- Stale lead detection: BullMQ delayed job fires after the configured follow_up_quiet_period; alerts PARTICIPANT 2 to follow up on quiet leads.
- Pipeline tracking: Status transitions (inquiry → quote sent → booked → completed) tracked in PhotoLead records.
- LLM tone shaping: Internal messages to PARTICIPANT 2 are organized and direct; drafted client messages are professional and warm.
