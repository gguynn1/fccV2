Required tools/dependencies:
- Node.js (LTS) with TypeScript (strict, ESM) for all runtime modules.
- `src/types.ts` for shared enums and cross-service vocabulary.
- Respect service and type boundaries: no direct runtime imports from sibling services.
- Local `types.ts` in this folder for scope-owned contracts.
- Asset, maintenance item, interval, and history types.
- Cycle-driven reminder logic based on last-performed dates and configured intervals.

Recommended tools/dependencies:
- `tsc --noEmit` to verify type boundaries.
- `eslint` to keep imports and unused symbols clean.
- `prettier` to keep docs and source formatting consistent.
- `vitest` for fixture-driven verification where the scope has behavior worth testing.
- Fixtures for due-date calculation, overdue detection, and history logging.

Technologies and integrations:
- Maintenance state persists in SQLite through the state service.
- Scheduler service can fire reminders when maintenance items approach their next-due date.
- Cross-topic link to vendors: when professional service is needed, the assistant can reference vendor history.
- Cross-topic link to finances: maintenance costs feed into expense tracking.
- No external service APIs — all data enters via conversation or email parsing.
