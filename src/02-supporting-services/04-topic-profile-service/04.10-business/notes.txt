Required tools/dependencies:
- Node.js (LTS) with TypeScript (strict, ESM) for all runtime modules.
- `src/types.ts` for shared enums and cross-service vocabulary.
- Respect service and type boundaries: no direct runtime imports from sibling services.
- Local `types.ts` in this folder for scope-owned contracts.
- Business lead-pipeline types for inquiries, draft replies, booking status, and quiet-period follow-up.
- Per-entity business profiles declaring business type and name.
- Approval-gated drafting support before anything is sent on behalf of the business owner.

Recommended tools/dependencies:
- `tsc --noEmit` to verify type boundaries.
- `eslint` to keep imports and unused symbols clean.
- `prettier` to keep docs and source formatting consistent.
- `vitest` for fixture-driven verification where the scope has behavior worth testing.
- Fixtures for new leads, stalled leads, and booking transitions across different business types.
- Tests that separate client-facing draft tone from internal assistant guidance.

Technologies and integrations:
- Business lead data persists in SQLite through the state service.
- Anthropic Claude API can draft or summarize lead communications before confirmation.
- Lead intake should come from email parsing or conversation rather than an external CRM.
- Each entity's business_type informs the tone the assistant uses when drafting client-facing messages.
