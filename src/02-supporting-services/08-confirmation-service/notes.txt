Required tools/dependencies:
- Node.js (LTS) with TypeScript (strict, ESM) for all runtime modules.
- `src/types.ts` for shared enums and cross-service vocabulary.
- Respect service and type boundaries: no direct runtime imports from sibling services.
- Local `types.ts` in this folder for scope-owned contracts.
- Pending confirmation storage keyed by thread, action, and expiry time.
- Reply-matching logic for approvals, rejections, and expirations.

Recommended tools/dependencies:
- `tsc --noEmit` to verify type boundaries.
- `eslint` to keep imports and unused symbols clean.
- `prettier` to keep docs and source formatting consistent.
- `vitest` for fixture-driven verification where the scope has behavior worth testing.
- Fixtures for short approval replies, structured choices, and expired confirmations.
- Tests that verify protected actions never auto-execute.

Technologies and integrations:
- Confirmation records persist through the state service into SQLite.
- Expiry handling pairs naturally with BullMQ delayed jobs and Redis-backed timers (AOF persistence required).
- Vitest flow coverage is the best way to verify approval, rejection, and expiration rules.
- Recovery after downtime: on startup, scan pending confirmations for any whose expires_at has passed during the outage — mark them expired and execute the on_expiry policy (notify user, offer reissue). Never auto-execute an expired confirmation.
