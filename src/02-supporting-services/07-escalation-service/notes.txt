Required tools/dependencies:
- Node.js (LTS) with TypeScript (strict, ESM) for all runtime modules.
- `src/types.ts` for shared enums and cross-service vocabulary.
- Respect service and type boundaries: no direct runtime imports from sibling services.
- Local `types.ts` in this folder for scope-owned contracts.
- Escalation state-machine logic tied to accountability level and timer state.
- Scheduler integration for follow-up deadlines and digest flags.

Recommended tools/dependencies:
- `tsc --noEmit` to verify type boundaries.
- `eslint` to keep imports and unused symbols clean.
- `prettier` to keep docs and source formatting consistent.
- `vitest` for fixture-driven verification where the scope has behavior worth testing.
- Clock-controlled tests for each escalation path.
- Fixtures that prove silence means different things by topic but never approval.

Technologies and integrations:
- Escalation logic should be implemented with XState v5.
- Timed escalation steps depend on BullMQ/Redis scheduling infrastructure (AOF persistence required).
- Escalation progress persists through the state service into SQLite.
- Recovery after downtime: on startup, reconcile active escalations — if a timer expired during downtime, advance to the next escalation step rather than firing the missed step late. Use the persisted next_action_at timestamp to determine what was missed.
