Required tools/dependencies:
- Node.js (LTS) with TypeScript (strict, ESM) for all runtime modules.
- `src/types.ts` for shared enums and cross-service vocabulary.
- Respect service and type boundaries: no direct runtime imports from sibling services.
- Local `types.ts` in this folder for scope-owned contracts.
- Budget counters for recent sends, pending batch windows, and per-thread collision checks.
- Priority decision outputs for send now, batch, or hold until the next digest.

Recommended tools/dependencies:
- `tsc --noEmit` to verify type boundaries.
- `eslint` to keep imports and unused symbols clean.
- `prettier` to keep docs and source formatting consistent.
- `vitest` for fixture-driven verification where the scope has behavior worth testing.
- Time-based simulations for quiet windows and high-volume days.
- Metrics or traces that explain why outbound items were combined or delayed.

Technologies and integrations:
- Budget counters should live in Redis shared with BullMQ (AOF persistence required — a crash without AOF resets all counters, risking duplicate sends on restart).
- Budget decisions consume recent state/history and return typed immediate, batched, or silent outcomes.
- Vitest fake timers are the best way to verify budget windows and message spacing.
- Recovery after restart: if counters are lost despite AOF, the budget service should reconstruct from recently_dispatched records in SQLite to avoid over-sending.
