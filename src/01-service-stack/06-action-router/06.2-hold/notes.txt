Required tools/dependencies:
- Respect service boundaries: no direct runtime imports from sibling services.
- Scheduler handoff contracts for batched items.
- Digest-window and quiet-window timing data for held outbound messages.

Recommended tools/dependencies:
- Time-based tests for morning digest, evening check-in, and daytime quiet behavior.
- Batch-merging fixtures that verify multiple items combine cleanly.

Technologies and integrations:
- Delayed jobs: BullMQ delayed jobs with hold_until timestamps — items sit in Redis until the next digest window opens.
- Batch aggregation: When the digest fires, query all held items for the target person/thread and merge into a single composed message.
- LLM composition: Anthropic Claude API to compose multiple held items into one natural digest message (tone shaped by topic profiles).
- Scheduler integration: BullMQ repeatable jobs for morning digest and evening check-in at configured times from the scheduler service.
- Testing: Vitest with fake timers; assert that held items accumulate and merge correctly at digest boundaries.
