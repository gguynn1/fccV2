Required tools/dependencies:
- A single queue item schema shared by human input, ingest events, and scheduled triggers.
- Timestamping, source tagging, and idempotency safeguards for inbound items.
- Producer and consumer interfaces that keep queue ownership clear.

Recommended tools/dependencies:
- Queue fixtures that mix human and system-generated events.
- Tracing or metrics around enqueue, dequeue, and retry behavior.

Technologies and integrations:
- Queue engine: BullMQ — Redis-backed job queue for Node.js with built-in retry, backoff, and delayed jobs.
- Backing store: Redis (v7+) as the shared data store for BullMQ; also used by scheduler and budget services.
- Idempotency: nanoid for unique queue item IDs; BullMQ's jobId deduplication to prevent double-processing.
- Serialization: Zod schemas for PendingQueueItem validation on enqueue; reject malformed items at the boundary.
- Observability: BullMQ event listeners (completed, failed, stalled) piped to pino structured logs.
- Dashboard: Bull Board (@bull-board/fastify) for dev/ops visibility into queue depth, failed jobs, and processing times.
- Scaling: Single-consumer design (one item at a time) matches the worker model; BullMQ supports concurrency if needed later.
- Alternative: For a lighter MVP, an in-process FIFO array with persistence to SQLite; graduate to BullMQ when Redis is available.
