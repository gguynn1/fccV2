Required tools/dependencies:
- Node.js (LTS) with TypeScript (strict, ESM) for all runtime modules.
- `src/types.ts` for shared enums and cross-service vocabulary.
- Respect service and type boundaries: no direct runtime imports from sibling services.
- Local `types.ts` in this folder for scope-owned contracts.
- A single queue item schema shared by human input, ingest events, and scheduled triggers.
- Timestamping, source tagging, and idempotency safeguards for inbound items.
- Producer and consumer interfaces that keep queue ownership clear.

Recommended tools/dependencies:
- `tsc --noEmit` to verify type boundaries.
- `eslint` to keep imports and unused symbols clean.
- `prettier` to keep docs and source formatting consistent.
- `vitest` for fixture-driven verification where the scope has behavior worth testing.
- Queue fixtures that mix human and system-generated events.
- Tracing or metrics around enqueue, dequeue, and retry behavior.

Technologies and integrations:
- Queue implementation: BullMQ backed by shared Redis.
- Redis persistence: AOF (appendonly yes) is required — a crash without AOF loses the entire queue.
- Queue items should be validated with Zod before enqueue and before worker consumption.
- BullMQ lifecycle events are a good place to emit pino logs for operational visibility.
- Retry: BullMQ exponential backoff for failed jobs; dead-letter queue for items that exhaust retries.
- Stale detection: on startup after downtime, pending items should be checked for staleness — items past their relevance window are logged silently, never dispatched late.
